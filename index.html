Dogs, Cats, Fish & Birds: Our Furry, Scaled and Feathered Friends

🐕

All About Dogs

Dogs (Canis lupus familiaris) are domesticated mammals, not natural wild animals. They were originally bred from wolves. They have been bred by humans for a long time, and were the first animals ever to be domesticated.

Dog Research

Various studies have shown that dogs provide significant positive health and wellness benefits. Moreover, the bond between humans and dogs dates back thousands of years, an interdependence that has evolved and strengthened over time.

🐱

All About Cats

Cats (Felis catus) are small, carnivorous mammals that are often kept as pets. They are one of humanity's oldest and most beloved pets.

Cat Research

Research indicates that owning cats or being around them could provide health benefits. These involve easing stress and anxiety, lowering blood pressure, and even reducing the risk of heart attack and stroke.

🐟

All About Fish

Fish are ecologically diverse and important for both recreational and commercial activities. Many people enjoy keeping fish as pets in aquariums for their beauty and intriguing behavior.

Fish Research

Research indicates that watching fish in an aquarium can significantly reduce one's stress levels and lower blood pressure, providing psychological benefits and contributing to overall well-being.

🐦

All About Birds

Birds are a group of warm-blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves, characterised by feathers, toothless beaked jaws, the laying of hard-shelled eggs, a high metabolic rate, a four-chambered heart, and a strong yet lightweight skeleton.

Bird Research

Studies show that observing and interacting with birds can induce relaxation and reduce stress and anxiety levels. They can also stimulate intellectual development through bird watching and related activities.
