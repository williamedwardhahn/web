    ðŸ”— Call for Applications

    Applications are currently being accepted for multidisciplinary Ph.D. program in Theoretical and Computational Neuroscience. The aim of this graduate program is to train scientists to perform cutting-edge brain research.

    Individuals with undergraduate degrees in any relevant discipline are invited to apply. Prior matriculants have had degrees in Physics, Mathematics, Philosophy, Computer Science, Cognitive Sciences, Engineering, Biology, Chemistry, Pharmacology, etc.

    ðŸ”— Application Information

    ðŸ”— Neuroscience Graduate Program

    Our application process is centralized by the Brain Institute at Florida Atlantic University. Applicants will be reviewed by the faculty in Complex Systems and Brain Sciences for consideration in the Ph.D. Theoretical and Computational Neuroscience.

    The application deadline is December 1st, annually.

    ðŸ”— Applicants with Existing Funding

    Applicants with existing funding (e.g., NSF GRFP; NIH F-31; DoE CSGF; DoD Smart; etc) are strongly encouraged to contact to prospective faculty prior to applying. Additionally, these applicants should mention their funding status in their personal statement.
